About Me

I am a junior at the university of Michigan, Ann Arbor pursuing a dual major in computer science and computer engineering.

One of my greatest areas of interest is video game design and development. I am an active member in the university's game development club, WolverineSoft, and participate in the club's regular game jams.

EECS 494:

EECS 494: Introduction to Game Development is an upper level computer science capstone course at the University of Michigan. It's stated goal is to give students enough familiarity and experience with development concepts and environments in one semester that they can be competitive with students who have had multiple years of game development education.

The class teaches important concepts from the basics of Unity and C# programming to more in-depth design patterns such as the observer pattern and event bus Pub/Sub. Learned skills are put to the test through three major projects. First is a retro game recreation, second is a shorter, design-oriented project, and third is a longer, more in-depth project, where large groups create and realize fully fledged, albeit small, games. More information about each project is/will be available on its page below.

 
  • Project 1: Metroid
  • Project 2: Inverse Dash
  • Project 3: Haunt
 

EECS 498:

EECS 498: Extended Reality and Society is an upper level computer science capstone course at the University of Michigan. It's is a sister class to EECS 494 in that it covers topics related to, but distinct from its counterpart. As the name suggests, this class focuses on learning the development environments associated with augmented and virtual reality. The class also distinguishes itself from EECS 494 in that it teaches Unreal engine for VR along with Unity for AR, allowing students to gain experience in multiple different engines.

EECS 498 is, similarly to 494, separated into three projects. First is a wholly VR project in which teams must recreate a beloved room from the university's Bob and Betty Beyster Building. Second is a wholly AR project in which teams create an app inspired by Pokemon GO. The final project, similarly to 494, is much larger in scope, but in this class, students must make an XR game or app that is more than just a video game. As above, more info is/will be available on the individual project pages below.

 
  • Project 1: CSE Simulator
  • Project 2: A2 GO!
  • Project 3: Dungeon Brawler VR
